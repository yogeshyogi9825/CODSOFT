Amul: The Taste of India

Amul is an acronym of the Indian Multinational cooperative society named Gujarat Milk Marketing Federation based in Anand, Gujarat. It is under the ownership of Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation Limited, Department of Cooperation, Government of Gujarat. It is controlled by 3.6 million milk producers.
Amul stands for Anand Milk Union Limited. It is a renowned Indian multinational cooperative society, headquartered in Anand, Gujarat. Here are some key points about Amul:

Founding and Ownership:

Founded on December 19, 1946, Amul emerged as a response to the exploitation of small dairy farmers by traders and agents. The farmers of Kaira (now Kheda) district, led by Tribhuvandas Patel, formed a cooperative to directly supply their milk to the Bombay Milk Scheme, bypassing unfair trade practices.
Today, Amul is under the ownership of the Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation Limited, which operates under the Department of Cooperation, Government of Gujarat. It is controlled by an impressive 3.6 million milk producers.
Overseas Ventures: Beyond India, Amul has ventured into overseas markets, spreading its influence globally.

The Amul Model: Amul’s dairy development model operates through a three-tiered structure: village-level dairy cooperative societies, district-level milk unions, and state-level federations. This model establishes a direct link between milk producers and consumers, eliminating middlemen
